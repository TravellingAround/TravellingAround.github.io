GREECE

Acropolis of Athens

The Acropolis of Athens is an ancient citadel located on a rocky outcrop above the city of Athens, Greece, and contains the remains of several ancient buildings of great architectural and historical significance, the most famous being the Parthenon. The word Acropolis is from the Greek words ἄκρον (akron, "highest point, extremity") and πόλις (polis, "city").The term acropolis is generic and there are many other acropoleis in Greece. During ancient times the Acropolis of Athens was also more properly known as Cecropia, after the legendary serpent-man Cecrops, the supposed first Athenian king.While there is evidence that the hill was inhabited as early as the fourth millennium BC, it was Pericles (c.495-429 BC) in the fifth century BC who coordinated the construction of the buildings whose present remains are the site's most important ones, including the Parthenon, the Propylaea, the Erechtheion and the Temple of Athena Nike.

Santorini

Santorini (Greek: Σαντορίνη) is an island ring composed of a group of volcanoes in the Aegean Sea, 200 kilometers southeast of the Greek mainland, located between 36.40° north latitude and 25.40° east longitude. The largest island in the Santorini ring is also called Santorini, also known as Thera (Θήρα). Santorini is located at the southernmost tip of the Cyclades Islands, covering an area of about 73 square kilometers and a population of about 14,000 people, mostly Greeks. "Santorini" was named by the Venetians in the 13th century, originating from Saint Irene (Italian: Santa Irene); before that, the island was called Thera, Kalisti (Καλλίστη, in ancient Greece Meaning "the most beautiful") or Strongiri (Στρογγύλη). The buildings on the island are blue and white, set against the blue sea, and are so beautiful that it is a famous tourist attraction. Administratively, Santorini belongs to the state of Thera.

Meteora

The Meteora (Greek: Μετέωρα) is a rock formation in the regional unit of Trikala, in Thessaly, Greece, hosting one of the largest and most precipitously built complexes of Eastern Orthodox monasteries, second in importance only to Mount Athos.The six (of an original twenty-four) monasteries are built on immense natural pillars and hill-like rounded boulders that dominate the local area. Between the 13th and 14th centuries, the twenty-four monasteries were established atop the rocks.Meteora is located near the town of Kalabaka at the northwestern edge of the Plain of Thessaly near the Pineios river and Pindus Mountains.

Cephalonia

Cephalonia is the site of the most visited section of the Kefalonia, located in the Ionian Sea, is the largest island in the Ionian Islands. The name of the island is believed to come from the ancient Greek mythological hero Cephalus (Κέφαλος). The island is famous as the home of Odysseus in Homer's Odyssey, who calls him "King of the Cephalonians". The story background of the best-selling novel and movie of the same name "Captain Corelli's Mandolin" (also translated as "War Lovers") is also set on the island that was occupied by the Germans during World War II. Today, the island's beautiful beaches and caves attract many tourists every year to spend their summers.
